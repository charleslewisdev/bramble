# Competitive Landscape

Research conducted 2026-03-09.

## Garden Planners (layout-focused)

### Planter
- Grid-based garden planner with companion planting data
- Planting times by zone, pest/disease info
- Very popular, paid tiers
- https://planter.garden/

### VegPlotter
- Free digital vegetable garden planner
- Drag-and-drop bed layout
- https://vegplotter.com/

### Seedtime
- Strong calendaring system — when to plant, transplant, harvest
- Desktop and mobile
- https://seedtime.us/

## Plant Care & Tracking

### Gardenize
- 45,000+ species database
- Photo journaling, care advice by climate/zone
- Subscription model

### PlantIn
- AI plant identification from photos
- Disease diagnosis, care reminders
- Subscription model

### Planta (user currently uses)
- Care reminders and notifications
- Plant care info database
- Good for individual pot tracking
- Limitation: doesn't handle whole-yard planning, zones, or garden layout

## All-in-One / Data-Rich

### Seed to Spoon
- Free, companion planting data
- Pest/disease/beneficial insect info
- Location-aware planting times
- https://www.seedtospoon.net/

### GrowIt!
- Social/community-driven
- Plant identification

## Farm Intelligence

### Trellis Dashboard
- Farm intelligence platform (commercial agriculture scale)
- Soil sensors, weather data, irrigation logging
- Not consumer/home garden focused
- https://app.trellis.org/

## Landscape Design

### iScape
- AR-based landscape visualization
- Focused on design, not ongoing management

### Realtime Landscaping Pro
- Desktop, one-time $280
- Professional landscape design

## Gaps We're Filling

1. **No single app** combines yard mapping + plant inventory + care calendar + notifications + AI research
2. **Subscription fatigue** — most good apps are SaaS ($5-10/mo)
3. **Self-hosted option doesn't exist** in this space
4. **Zone-based thinking** — most apps track individual plants, not garden zones/beds as first-class entities
5. **LLM integration** — no garden app uses AI for contextual advice ("what's wrong with this plant?" with photo + your specific conditions)
6. **External data integration** — weather, sun position, daylight hours, property data are typically manual entry
7. **Notification flexibility** — most apps only notify through their own mobile app
